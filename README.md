Why It's Different from Traditional Braces
The conventional wisdom in knee bracing has long been centred on stability. Traditional hinged braces, rigid supports, and even most soft compression sleeves operate on the same basic principle: constrain the joint, limit unwanted movement, and prevent the knee from collapsing under load. This approach has genuine merit for certain conditions — particularly post-surgical stabilisation or ligament injury — but it comes with significant trade-offs in terms of comfort, mobility, and long-term wearability.
Nübrace's central claim is that it represents a fundamentally different philosophy. Rather than immobilising the knee, the PowerBoost™ spring system is designed to actively improve mobility and performance. The brace does not merely hold the knee still; it assists its natural movement cycle. This distinction is not merely semantic. For a person with knee osteoarthritis who needs to walk several miles a day, the difference between a brace that restricts movement and one that facilitates it is the difference between something they will actually wear and something that ends up in a drawer.
Traditional braces also rely heavily on strap tension to generate their stabilising effect. This can create pressure points, restrict circulation, and become uncomfortable over the course of a long day. Nübrace's spring-loading distributes the assistive force more evenly across the joint, reducing reliance on strap compression alone. The straps are still present and still important for fit and alignment, but they are not the primary mechanism of action.
